What We Do

FORCE programs focus on equipping, informing and empowering marginalized and vulnerable Persons with sustainable community-based models that propel the attainment of high level of self-reliance and prosperity among the youth, Women and community at large.

Institutional Capacity Development

The program focuses on building the institutional capacity of FORCE and other partner community-based organizations in administration and financial management, networking and partnerships including resource mobilization.

Child and youth development

The program is dedicated to advancing child development and youth development by ensuring that children and young people not only survive but also thrive and succeed

Education

FORCE is dedicated to ensuring that every child and young person can access high-quality primary education, fostering their development into valuable, contributing members of their communities

Good governance and Human Rights

The Good Governance and Human Rights program by FORCE is dedicated to cultivating peaceful, just, and inclusive communities that are resilient against violence, abuse, and exploitation.

Environmental management

FORCE conducts environmental education majoring on waste disposal and renewable energy, Promoting environmental conservation for a clean and greener tomorrow (Climate Protection)

Sustainable Livelihoods and food security

FORCE is dedicated to enhancing the socio-economic well-being of rural communities by increasing productivity through the adoption of improved seeds and agricultural practices.

Public Health and WASH

In the realm of public health and WASH (Water, Sanitation, and Hygiene), FORCE directs its efforts towards two key areas: HIV/AIDS, Water and Sanitation

Our Portfolio

FORCE has made remarkable strides in the socio-economic development of youth in Eastern Uganda, achieving several notable accomplishments. By implementing targeted vocational training programs, FORCE has equipped young people with essential skills in trades such as carpentry, tailoring, and agriculture, significantly enhancing their employability and self-sufficiency. Additionally, the organization has facilitated access to microfinance and entrepreneurial training, enabling youth to start and sustain their own businesses, thereby stimulating local economies and reducing poverty rates.

International youth forum.

The German Federal Ministry for Economic Cooperation and Development (BMZ), together with the BMZ Youth Advisory Council, organized an International Youth Forum from 21 November until 27 November 2022 in Berlin to which around 20 young people from partner countries of German development cooperation were selected to participate in this Youth Forum.

The aim of the Youth Forum was a joint exchange and mutual learning about opportunities and challenges for youth participation, based on the experiences of young people from partner countries of German development cooperation.
